Istan Rev (2020) Should all Archives be Open Access. Accessible at https://direct.mit.edu/books/oa-edited-volume/4933/chapter/625171/Accessing-the-Past-or-Should-Archives-Provide-Open. (Accessed: 27th November 2025).

Should all Archives be Open Access- Response

After reading this article i can understand the positives and negatives of open access archives. One of Revs points are that while open access to information is a positive it is also negative as well due to the dangers associated with it from an ethical and privacy concern. Open archives may contain personal information which can lead to an increase in personal data being shared online without the consent of the person. The Open archives may lead to ethical problems as well such as misinformation which can skew events which can give people a false event of what happened creating bias and propaganda if written in a way to provoke an certain opinion of any one thing.

In Revs second point he argues that archives that become easily accessible become unreliable and lose authenticity . This is true in some cases as open access can lead to manipulation or false information which takes away power from archives. In my opinion Rev is making a point that open access can strengthen historical accountability and confidence if archives are managed properly and efficiently by getting them fact checked by credible historians to prevent the spread of misinformation.

In the last point Rev makes the point that open archives doesn't just include files online but questions of stuff such as infostructure, institutions and values. In other regions like Central Europe it can lead to complications in open access.
